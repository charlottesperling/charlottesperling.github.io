Daughter Album Art

Creating a multidimensional experience

Background: Daughter, an up and coming folk artist, needed a graphic designer to create limited addition album art and pack-aging for her album, The Wild Youth. With digital media dominating the market, Daughter needed innovative packaging that would convince consumers to pick up and purchase a CD in a brick and mortar store. Solution: The designer decided to create album art that functions as a metaphor for the loss of innocence, the most prevalent theme found in The Wild Youth. The album’s cover features the silhouette of a small child, which alludes to youth and innocence; however, once heat is applied (through applied pressure, sunlight, fire, etc.) the silhouette gives way to reveal a skull. The skull represents loss of innocence; the application of heat demonstrates the journey from childlike innocence to the loss of it. Any time heat is applied, the packaging ‘ages’. As part of her process, the designer always begins with research; research informs the designer of what trends are currently on the market, and helps spark inspiration. Research for Daughter’s album artwork involved investigation into different materials that could work for the final concept. The final solution involved thermal chromatic paint that reacts to heat. Heat activated paint is not a common medium; therefore, there is not much literature in regards to its use. The designer tracked down a little known vendor, and received a packet of thermal chromatic powder. Through trial and error, an appropriate mixing medium ratio was developed. Following research, the designer thumbnails various concepts, and later moves to develop the final concept. For The Wild Youth, the designer executed a number of illustrations, but ultimately decided to only implement the skull illustration in the final solution.
